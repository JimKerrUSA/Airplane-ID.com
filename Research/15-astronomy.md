# Astronomy & Sky Identification Apps

## Category Overview

Astronomy apps use the camera and device sensors to identify stars, constellations, planets, satellites, and other celestial objects. These apps typically employ AR overlays and gyroscope integration.

## Unique Features in Astronomy Apps

1. **AR Overlay:** Camera view with labeled celestial objects
2. **Gyroscope Integration:** Point device = identify
3. **Time Travel:** See sky at past/future dates
4. **Satellite Tracking:** ISS, Hubble passes
5. **Constellation Art:** Mythological figures overlaid
6. **Telescope Control:** Connect to physical telescopes
7. **Offline Mode:** Works without internet

---

## UI/UX Patterns Observed

1. **360 Sky Dome:** Full spherical sky representation
2. **AR Camera Mode:** Labels overlaid on live view
3. **Time Slider:** Navigate through time
4. **Object Cards:** Tap for detailed information
5. **Night Mode:** Red-tinted UI for dark adaptation
6. **Compass Integration:** Direction indicators
7. **Altitude/Azimuth:** Coordinate displays

---

## Key Takeaways for Product Development

1. **AR is the killer feature** - Point-and-identify is core UX
2. **Gyroscope critical** - Device orientation sensing essential
3. **Offline works** - No internet needed once downloaded
4. **One-time purchase option** - Sky Guide, SkySafari show alternative
5. **Night mode important** - Red UI for dark adaptation
6. **Education focus** - Learning about space is core value
7. **Satellite tracking popular** - ISS passes drive engagement
